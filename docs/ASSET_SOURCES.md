# KAISETSU — Asset Sources Reference

> Authoritative, deduped compilation of verified asset research from eight discovery agents (itch.io, OpenGameArt, CraftPix/Kenney marketplaces, plus dedicated character/boss finders and a licensing/creators finder).
> **Scope:** 2D **side-scroller** (side-view platformer — NOT top-down) soulslike, Godot 4.6, pixel art. Cold, desaturated, melancholic mood (deep blues, slate greys, sickly greens, muted purples; sparse warm accents). Tiles authored ~32px; characters multi-tile ~48–64px.
> **Every asset below was WebFetch-verified** for title/creator/price/license/projection. Only verified items are recommendations; unverified leads are segregated in §6.
> **Recurring caveats apply to almost everything:** most packs are 16×16 (scale 2× cleanly toward the ~32px target) and most ship a warmer/brighter palette than KAISETSU's cold target (plan a desaturation/recolor pass — prefer packs shipping PSD/ASE/Aseprite sources or a color ramp).

## 4. License Cheat-Sheet (Commercial Indie Game)

| License | Commercial? | Attribution? | Verdict | Notes / gotchas |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **CC0** (Public Domain) | ✅ Yes | ❌ None | **BEST — use freely** | LuizMelo, Kenney, much of ansimuz, Jetrel, M13, Shackhal. Gotcha: CC0 may cover art but NOT bundled music/fonts — check each file. Keep a private provenance record anyway. |
| **CC-BY 4.0 / 3.0** | ✅ Yes | ✅ **Mandatory** | Safe **with credit** | Credit creator name + link, note if modified. e.g. `Tileset by o_lobster — https://o-lobster.itch.io/ (CC-BY 4.0)`. Applies to: o_lobster, chierit, Chad Wolfe, OPP (pick the CC-BY option). Forgetting the credit = license breach (#1 mistake). |
| **OGA-BY** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Mandatory | Safe with credit | Treat exactly like CC-BY. Common on OGA, usually dual-licensed — pick the most permissive offered. |
| **itch single-purchase / "standard"** | ✅ Yes (per page) | Usually no (varies) | Safe to ship; **read each page** | NOT one legal standard — each creator writes their own. Common: commercial OK, modify OK, **no resale/redistribution of the raw assets**, increasingly **no AI training**. "Free / name-your-price" does NOT mean public domain. Re-check license.txt inside each ZIP. |
| **CraftPix** | ✅ Yes (free + premium) | ❌ None | Safe to ship | No resale of source files; explicit **no-AI/ML-training** clause. Membership downloads stay usable after sub ends. Freebies are fine for commercial GAME use (the FREE-projects-only limit targets enterprise resale/courses). |
| **CC-BY-SA** (ShareAlike) | ✅ but viral | ✅ Mandatory | ⚠️ **AVOID** | ShareAlike forces your derivative under CC-BY-SA — legally murky for a closed-source commercial game. Affects: Sidescroller Beach, PlatForge Castle, rgsdev Knights, OPP if you pick the SA option. Prefer CC-BY/CC0 instead. |
| **GPL** | — | — | ⚠️ **AVOID for art** | Copyleft software license; can be argued to force your whole game GPL. Fine for tools, dangerous for shipped art. |
| **"Non-commercial only"** | ❌ No | — | ⛔ **DISQUALIFYING** | Never use in KAISETSU without buying a separate commercial license. Watch for this hidden in otherwise-free itch packs. |

**Practical rules:** (a) Prefer CC0 and "commercial-OK, no-attribution" itch/CraftPix packs. (b) Maintain a single **CREDITS/licenses ledger** from day one — asset, creator, URL, license, attribution-owed. (c) Avoid CC-BY-SA and GPL art. (d) Re-verify the license inside each downloaded ZIP — store page and bundled file occasionally differ; bundled music/fonts often carry different terms.

**Per-pack flags to track if shipping:** o_lobster & chierit packs = **attribution required**. aamatniekss / CraftPix / Kevin D. Eternal = **no-AI clause** (don't feed to generators). ansimuz GothicVania Church = license **unclear on page** (verify CC0 in readme). The Flavare FREE tier forbids modification + mandatory credit (buy Premium to recolor). Penusbmic Ancient Caves = license not restated on its page (infer from siblings/bundle).

## 5. Recommended Buy / Style Strategy

**Pick ONE cohesive direction so all six zones read as one world.** Mixing creators causes pixel-density and palette clashes that are expensive to reconcile later.

**Primary recommendation — the Penusbmic DARK Series Bundle ($59.99) as the environment spine.** It is the single best value-plus-fit: one tonally-consistent dark/melancholic side-scroller family that already covers **Z3 (Ancient Caves), Z4 (Mountain Pass + Sacred Temple), Z5 (Castle of Bones), and Z1 (City)** — i.e. most of the game in one buy, already on the cold-mood target.

**Fill the gaps the bundle doesn't cover:**
- **Z2 jungle:** Szadi **Pixel Fantasy Deep Forest** ($2.40) or Sebbyspoons **Moonlit Forest** ($3.99).
- **Z3 crystals (upgrade):** aamatniekss **Fantasy Caves** ($12) + CraftPix **Free Crystal Cave Backgrounds** for the cold crystal look.
- **Z0 coastal / Hub harbor (the real gap):** ansimuz **Magic Cliffs** (CC0) as a coast base + **bespoke ship/dock props via PixelLab** (`create_sidescroller_tileset`) or commission. No verified pack fills this.
- **Backdrops everywhere:** dotbioma **Parallax Backgrounds** ($1.50, editable ASE for cold recolor).

**Alternative single-creator spine** if you prefer itch's "Hi-Bit" look with bundled enemies: **aamatniekss** (Caves→Z3, Foggy Cliffs→Z4, Jungle→Z2, Castle Dungeon→Z5) — most packs ship a player + themed enemy. Or **Szadi** for the cheapest cross-compatible line. Either way, **commit to one** and recolor the rest toward deep-blue/slate/sickly-green/muted-purple.

**Characters:** standardize on **LuizMelo (CC0)** for the cohesive hero+enemy+boss roster (Martial Hero → Sōji base; Monsters Creatures Fantasy → enemies; Evil Wizard 2 → Hollow Magistrate; Dark Knight → Royal Guard), then add **chierit** bosses (credit required) for the big phase-change spectacle fights.

**Fit alongside existing PixelLab assets:** The user already generates Sōji (player) and a sea-stone tileset in PixelLab. Treat **PixelLab as the source of truth for the protagonist and for Zone 0 / Hub** (where the market has no good fit — generate the wrecked ship, broken docks, harbor pier props there). Use the **purchased packs for the bulk environment + enemy/boss roster**, and **recolor every pack to the PixelLab palette** so the generated Sōji reads as the warm-toned focal point against a consistent cold world. Off-the-shelf hero sprites here are recolor bases/animation references, not replacements for the PixelLab Sōji.
